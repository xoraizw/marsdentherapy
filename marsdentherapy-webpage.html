Learn About Our Therapy Approach

At Marsden Therapy, we specialize in providing Speech, Occupational, Psychology and PBS/BSP services for children. Our main goal is to empower families with the tools they need to make informed decisions about their child’s healthcare. 

We recognize that Speech, Occupational, Psychology and Positive Behaviour support are ongoing processes that go well beyond the scope of our one-hour therapy sessions. Children actually spend the majority of their time outside of the therapy setting, where they encounter difficulties and experiences from everyday life. Because of this, we take a holistic approach, concentrating not only on the therapeutic requirements of the person, but also on teaching and mentoring parents and caregivers.

We equip parents and caregivers with the necessary information, abilities, and resources to incorporate therapeutic treatments into daily life. This strategy successfully converts the remaining 23/7 into priceless opportunities for growth and learning.

Psychology and PBS : A Path to Improved Mental Health

At Marsden Therapy, we are dedicated to supporting children who are finding it difficult to adjust to changes in their lives. Change can be overwhelming for young people, and they may struggle to understand or adapt to new situations. This may include transitioning to a new school, adjusting to shifts within the family, or learning new skills as they grow.

To support children through these challenges, we provide two core services: Psychology and Positive Behaviour Support (PBS).

Our Psychology team helps children understand how their thoughts, emotions, and experiences influence their behaviour and wellbeing. Through evidence-based therapeutic approaches, our Psychologists support children in recognising and expressing emotions, building coping skills, managing anxiety and stress, improving emotional regulation, and developing resilience and confidence. This service is particularly beneficial for children experiencing emotional difficulties, behavioural changes, or challenges related to mental health and adjustment.

Our Positive Behaviour Support (PBS) service focuses on understanding the reasons behind behaviours of concern and creating practical, strengths-based strategies to support positive change. We use a holistic and person-centred approach to identify what a child is communicating through their behaviour and work toward building new skills that improve their daily life. PBS is especially helpful for children with developmental delays, autism spectrum disorder, or challenges related to communication, regulation, or behaviour. Our approach includes functional behaviour assessments, identifying triggers and patterns, teaching replacement and adaptive skills, supporting emotional regulation, and working with families, carers, and educators to ensure consistent strategies.

Together, our Psychology and PBS services help children feel supported, understood, and empowered as they navigate change and develop lifelong skills.
